About Ichiko Aoba

Founder of independent label hermine.

Ichiko Aoba is a Japanese singer-songwriter, known for her unique blend of folk, classical, and ambient music. Born in 1990, Ichiko began playing classical guitar at the age of 17, and her ethereal, introspective sound has since captivated audiences around the world. She has released several studio albums, collaborated with various artists, and contributed music to films, video games, and theater productions.

Her music transports listeners into an intimate dreamscape, drawing inspiration from nature, childhood memories, and fantasy. Aoba’s sound is often described as peaceful and calming, creating a serene atmosphere in which listeners can reflect and lose themselves.

Since her 2010 debut, she has released over seven albums, and has traveled across the globe with nothing more than her voice and nylon-string guitar. Since releasing Windswept Adan (2020), described as a “soundtrack for a fictional film,” she has also begun to incorporate strings in her live shows.

Aoba’s work as a radio presenter, narrator, film composer, and performer across a wide variety of fields has consistently won her acclaim, including the prestigious best soundtrack award at the 77th Mainichi Film Awards for her work on Amiko (2022). Recipient of the ANCHOR Award at the 2023 Reeperbahn Festival.
